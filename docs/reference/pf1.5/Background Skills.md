# Background Skills (PF1.5)

> **Status:** Active campaign rule.
> **Source:** Adapted from *Pathfinder Unchained* (Paizo, 2015) — Background Skills optional system.
> **Campaign deviation:** Level 1 grants 4 background ranks instead of 2.

---

## Overview

The Background Skills optional system splits Pathfinder skills into two categories: **adventuring skills** and **background skills**. Every character gains a separate small pool of ranks to spend on background skills, allowing rich non-combat backstory without sacrificing combat-relevant skill investment.

In a memory-arc campaign like *Echoes of Baphomet's Fall*, background skills carry extra weight — they represent the lives the PCs led *before* the amnesia. The richer the background, the more the memory recovery beats land throughout the campaign.

---

## Core Rule

**All characters gain 4 background skill ranks at character creation (level 1), then 2 background skill ranks per level thereafter.**

- Background ranks do not benefit from the Intelligence modifier.
- Background ranks can only be spent on background skills.
- Standard adventuring skill ranks (class + Int per level) may be spent on either adventuring OR background skills at player discretion.
- The campaign uses the standard PF1 skill maximum: a character cannot have more ranks in any single skill than their character level.

**Level-1 distribution constraint:** Because the skill-rank cap at level 1 is 1 rank per skill, the 4 starting background ranks must be distributed across 4 different background skills. This is a feature — it forces breadth of background at character creation rather than depth in one area.

---

## Why the Level-1 Deviation from RAW

The official *Pathfinder Unchained* rule grants 2 background ranks per level uniformly, including level 1. This campaign grants 4 at level 1 instead. The reasoning:

1. **Character creation pain point.** At level 1, RAW gives only 2 ranks to distribute across 13 possible background skills. Players feel forced to abandon most of their character concept. Doubling the starting budget addresses this without affecting later progression.
2. **Memory-arc fit.** Background skills represent pre-amnesia identity. Giving players more starting budget to express that identity serves the campaign's narrative goals — the more pre-amnesia life is on the sheet, the more the memory beats ache when they arrive.
3. **Mechanical impact is negligible.** The total delta over 20 levels is +2 ranks. Background skills don't affect combat, so this has no balance impact.

## New Skills

### Artistry (Int)

Like Craft, Perform, and Profession, Artistry is a number of separate skills — each focused on creating a non-physical creative work. Examples: Artistry (musical composition), Artistry (poetry), Artistry (playwriting), Artistry (choreography), Artistry (philosophy), Artistry (criticism).

The difference from Craft: Craft makes a physical object; Artistry makes the *idea* or *pattern* itself. A composer's score is Artistry; the printed sheet music is Craft. A playwright's script is Artistry; the bound book is Craft.

**Class skill:** Any class that has Craft or Perform as a class skill also has Artistry as a class skill.

**Common uses:** Earning a living as an artist, creating commissioned works, building reputation as a creator. See the *Pathfinder Unchained* Artistry rules for the full earning/commission table.

### Lore (Int, Trained Only)

A specialized knowledge skill, narrower than the standard Knowledge skills. Examples: Lore (Backar Forest), Lore (Molthuni Imperial politics), Lore (Korvosa harbor district), Lore (taverns in a region), Lore (Council of Everbloom rumors).

**Scope rule:** Lore must be narrower than the most relevant Knowledge skill. The broader the scope, the shallower the knowledge.

| Appropriate Lore | Too Broad |
|---|---|
| Lore (Canorate Vendor Quarter) | Lore (Canorate) |
| Lore (Backar Forest druids) | Lore (Druids) |
| Lore (Molthuni Imperial bureaucracy) | Lore (Molthune) |
| Lore (Lake Encarthen shipping routes) | Lore (Shipping) |
| Lore (Iomedaean liturgy) | Lore (Iomedae) |

**Class skill:** Lore is always a class skill for all characters.

**Bardic interaction:** For Bardic Knowledge, Lore Master, and similar class features, Lore counts as a Knowledge skill — but only Lore skills in which the character is trained.

**Use in play:** Lore checks use the same DC scale as Knowledge checks (DC 10 easy, DC 15 basic, DC 20-30 tough). At GM discretion, a partially-related Lore skill may substitute for a Knowledge check at a -5 penalty.

## Campaign-Specific Notes

**Lore skills as memory anchors:** A character with Lore (their hometown), Lore (their pre-amnesia profession's specialty), or Lore (a place or topic from their backstory) creates explicit hooks for memory recovery beats. The GM may use these as triggers for sensory-echo moments — a familiar smell, an unexplained competence, a strange sense of déjà vu when the topic comes up in play.

**Profession as a backstory anchor:** A character with high Profession (sailor) who awakens in Canorate with no memory of their nautical past has a built-in mystery. The skill works mechanically; the *why* is plot.

**Artistry for creative-class characters:** Bards, poets, and similar character concepts benefit substantially from Artistry as a class skill. A Bard's pre-amnesia compositions may still exist somewhere in the world — and may be recognized by NPCs who remember the artist but not their face.

---

## Quick Reference for Players

> **Background Skills Quick Rule:** You get 4 background skill ranks at level 1, then 2 per level thereafter. These ranks can only be spent on the Background Skills list (Appraise, Artistry, Craft, Handle Animal, Knowledge engineering/geography/history/nobility, Linguistics, Lore, Perform, Profession, Sleight of Hand). Standard class skill ranks can be spent on either pool — adventuring or background. Two new skills: **Artistry** (Int, creative work) and **Lore** (Int, narrow specialized knowledge — always a class skill for everyone).
